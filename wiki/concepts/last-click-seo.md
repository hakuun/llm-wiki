# Last-Click SEO

Last-Click SEO is the goal of making a search result page satisfy the searcher's task so well that the user does not return to the search results to click another page or reformulate the query.

## Meaning

From the user's perspective, the page was the last click because it solved the problem. From the search engine's perspective, that behavior can indicate that the page matched the query's intent and delivered value.

## Practical Implication

For AI tool sites, Last-Click SEO means a page should be designed as a task-completion surface, not only a content page. Depending on intent, it may need:

- A usable tool or demo.
- A clear workflow.
- Screenshots and examples.
- Pricing or API information.
- Comparisons and alternatives.
- FAQ and edge-case coverage.
- A direct conversion path.

## Limits

The source discusses behavioral signals such as depth, dwell time, and search behavior as possible indicators. Treat these as directional SEO reasoning rather than a confirmed disclosure of search-engine ranking mechanics.
